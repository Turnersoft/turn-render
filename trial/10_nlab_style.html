Idea

A group is a fundamental structure in algebra that captures the abstract essence of symmetry. Groups appear throughout mathematics as the automorphism groups of mathematical objects, encoding all the ways an object can be transformed while preserving its essential structure.

Groups are precisely the objects in the category Grp whose morphisms are group homomorphisms. This categorical viewpoint unifies group theory with the broader mathematical landscape.

Definition

Definition (Group)

A group is a set G equipped with:

  • a binary operation Œº: G √ó G ‚Üí G (often written as multiplication Œº(g,h) = gh)
  • an identity element e ‚àà G
  • an inverse operation i: G ‚Üí G (written i(g) = g^{-1})

such that the following axioms hold:

  1. Associativity: (gh)k = g(hk) for all g,h,k ‚àà G
  2. Unit law: eg = ge = g for all g ‚àà G
  3. Inverse law: gg^{-1} = g^{-1}g = e for all g ‚àà G
Remark

The axioms ensure that every element has a unique left inverse and a unique right inverse, and these coincide. The identity element is also unique by the unit laws.

Categorical perspective

From the categorical point of view, a group can be understood in several equivalent ways:

A group is a groupoid with a single object. This perspective emphasizes that groups are about composition of transformations.
Theorem (Cayley)

Every group G is isomorphic to a subgroup of the symmetric group Sym(G) on its underlying set. This is via the regular representation G ‚Üí Sym(G) given by g ‚Ü¶ (h ‚Ü¶ gh).

Groups form a category Grp where:

  • Objects: groups
  • Morphisms: group homomorphisms
  • Composition: composition of functions
  • Identities: identity homomorphisms

Properties

Theorem (Lagrange)

If H is a subgroup of a finite group G, then |H| divides |G|. The quotient |G|/|H| is called the index of H in G.

The proof uses the fact that cosets partition the group into disjoint subsets of equal size. This is a fundamental example of how group actions (here, the action of H on G by left multiplication) lead to structural insights.
